Welcome to My World

“I am the echo in empty rooms
the silence after my last message.”

I once bought new notebooks. Thought I’d fill them with poems and plans. They’re still blank. I sleep beside them sometimes as if they’ll write my healing for me.

I made a playlist called “better days.” It stops at track three. I haven’t pressed play in months. Some songs are too honest.

I promised I’d visit. Said we’d laugh till sunrise, like we used to. But I kept choosing sleep over people, distance over effort. I let the silence win.

I typed out a message three paragraphs long. It said everything. I backspaced it all. No one needs to carry my chaos.

I told them I was catching up on shows, writing again. But the truth? I just laid there, staring at the ceiling cracks, connecting them like constellations of failure.

Every unread message is an anchor. Every paused episode, a moment I’ll never live again. Grief isn't always loud. Sometimes it’s just stillness.

I keep telling myself I’ll come back to the people, the dreams, the version of me I abandoned. But most nights, I just scroll and pretend I’m okay with fading quietly.

“Maybe in another life,
I stayed.
I pressed send.
I healed.
Here, I am just disappearing.”
Notebooks are tombstones of things I never said. Playlists are eulogies for who I used to be. Messages are confessions I couldn’t deliver. Silence is my cruelest companion. Memories are ghosts I sleep beside.
I whispered to the night “stay” but it turned its back, and I was alone again.
